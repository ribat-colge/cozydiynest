Rug Size Cheat Sheet

Rugs are not just decor pieces — they define how a room feels. The right rug size creates warmth, balance, and a sense of quiet luxury, while the wrong size can make even the most beautiful space feel unfinished.

This guide will help you choose the perfect rug size for your living room or bedroom with confidence and clarity.

← Back to Rugs Collection

1) Always Anchor the Seating Area

In living rooms, rugs should visually connect the furniture. When a rug is too small, the space feels scattered and uncomfortable. A well-sized rug creates a clear seating zone that feels intentional and inviting.

A simple rule designers follow: at least the front legs of sofas and chairs should rest on the rug.

2) 8×10 Rugs: The Safe, Cozy Choice

An 8×10 rug is ideal for most medium-sized living rooms. It balances the space without overwhelming it and works beautifully with sectionals, sofas, and accent chairs.

This size is perfect if you want a cozy feel while keeping the room open and breathable.

3) 9×12 Rugs: When You Want a Luxury Look

Larger rooms benefit from larger rugs. A 9×12 rug allows all furniture pieces to sit fully on the rug, creating a polished, high-end appearance.

This is the size designers often use in magazine-worthy living rooms.

4) Bedroom Rugs Should Feel Soft and Calm

In bedrooms, rugs are all about comfort. The most practical placement is under the lower two-thirds of the bed, giving you softness exactly where you need it.

This setup keeps the room visually balanced while adding warmth underfoot.

5) Create a Hotel-Style Bedroom Feel

For a more luxurious bedroom, choose a rug that extends beyond both sides of the bed. This creates symmetry and instantly makes the space feel calm and refined.

Pair it with soft lighting and neutral bedding for a timeless, cozy look.

6) Common Rug Size Mistakes to Avoid

The most common mistake is choosing a rug that’s too small. Small rugs visually shrink a room and break the flow between furniture.

When in doubt, always choose the larger size — it creates a more expensive and thoughtfully designed look.
